Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is Machine Learning?

    Machine Learning is a branch of artificial intelligence dedicated to guiding robots in their Scientists and engineers hope that machine learning will eventually help machines make unguided choices by independently interpreting data from the world around them. Algorithms use this information to construct instructions that will be transformed into actions by artificial intelligence applications.

  • What is Big Data?

    It refers to a very large set of data that no conventional database management or information management tool can really work. It is a concept to store a huge amount of information on a digital basis, and allows, in a new order of magnitude never before achieved, to capture, search, share, store, analyze and present Datas.

  • Data Warehouse, what is it?

    A Data Warehouse is a relational database that is designed and built for data queries and analytics, decision making, and Business Intelligence-related activities, rather than transaction processing or other traditional database uses.

  • Data Warehouse vs Data Lake: what are the differences ?

    A Data Warehouse collects a large amount of data accumulated within a business from different data sources. It is used to make decisions. Data Lake, on the other hand, is a storage bank that holds a huge amount of raw data in its original format until the business needs it.

  • What is Data Mining?

    Data Mining is a process to find correlations or patterns between many relational databases. It relies on complex and sophisticated mathematical algorithms to segment data and evaluates future probabilities. This process allows users to analyze data from different angles, categorize data, and summarize the identified relationships.
